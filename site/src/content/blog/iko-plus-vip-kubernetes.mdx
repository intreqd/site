In the world of InterSystems IRIS mirroring, the **Virtual IP (VIP)** is a cornerstone of high availability. It provides a single, consistent IP address that always points to the current primary member. But in Kubernetes, where IPs are ephemeral and managed by services, how do we implement a true VIP?

### The Challenge: Kubernetes vs. Traditional VIPs

Kubernetes Services (like `LoadBalancer`) provide a stable entry point, but they don't always align perfectly with the "gratuitous ARP" behavior that IRIS expects for its VIP. We want a solution that works natively with the IKO and provides the same experience as traditional bare-metal or VM deployments.

### The Solution: Kube-vip

**Kube-vip** provides a high-availability and load-balancing solution for Kubernetes clusters. It can manage a virtual IP address and associate it with specific nodes or pods, making it perfect for our IRIS mirroring needs.

### Step 1: Deploying Kube-vip

We deploy Kube-vip as a DaemonSet in our cluster. We configure it to monitor our IRIS pods and manage the specified VIP.

Once deployed, we can see the Kube-vip pods running across our nodes:

### Step 2: Assigning the VIP

We specify the desired VIP in our configuration. Kube-vip will ensure this IP is assigned to the node currently hosting the primary IRIS member.

![VIP Assigned to Node](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12153).png)

### Step 3: Testing Failover

The real test of a VIP is what happens during a failover. We'll run a continuous `curl` loop against our management portal via the VIP while triggering a mirror failover.

```yaml
# Test pod definition
apiVersion: v1
kind: Pod
metadata:
  name: curl-test
spec:
  containers:
    - name: curl-loop
      image: curlimages/curl:8.10.1
      command: ["/bin/sh", "-c", "while true; do curl -sk -o /dev/null -w '%{http_code}\n' https://192.168.1.152:52774/; sleep 5; done"]
```

Watch the magic as the VIP moves seamlessly from the old primary to the new primary with minimal disruption:

![VIP Failover Demo](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/screen_capture_select-area_20251112065143_anim_preview.jpg)

🎉 Success! We have a true, functional VIP in Kubernetes.

### Summary

By leveraging **Kube-vip** alongside the **InterSystems Kubernetes Operator**, we can bring traditional high-availability patterns into the cloud-native era. This ensures that clients and integrations always have a reliable, consistent way to reach the primary IRIS member, regardless of where it's currently running in the cluster.
