In this installment of the **IKO Plus** series, we are looking at **Karmada** (Kubernetes Armada). If you are managing InterSystems IRIS across dozens or even hundreds of Kubernetes clusters, you need a way to centralize management and propagate resources reliably.

### Why Karmada?

Karmada allows you to run your cloud-native applications across multiple Kubernetes clusters and clouds, with no changes to your applications. It provides:
- **Centralized Management**: One control plane to rule them all.
- **Resource Propagation**: Define a resource once, and Karmada ensures it exists where it should.
- **Cross-Cluster Governance**: Ensure compliance across your entire fleet.

We have our "host" cluster where Karmada is installed, and we want to join our regional clusters.

### Summary & Advanced Features

Karmada scratches the surface of what's possible with multi-cluster management. Advanced users can explore:
- **Overrides**: Customize resources per joined cluster (e.g., different storage classes).
- **Networking**: Integrate with Submariner for cross-cluster pod-to-pod communication.
- **Push vs. Pull**: Choose the configuration style that fits your workflow.

[![Karmada GitHub](https://gh-card.dev/repos/karmada-io/karmada.png)](https://karmada.io/)

Scaling InterSystems IRIS is no longer a cluster-by-cluster manual effort. With Karmada and the IKO, you have a true "Armada" of data platforms at your fingertips.
