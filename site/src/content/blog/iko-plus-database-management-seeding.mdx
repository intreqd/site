In this installment of **IKO Plus**, we're tackling the critical task of **Database Management**. Specifically, how do you handle migrations or "Day 1" seeding of your data into a brand new, mirrored InterSystems IrisCluster on Kubernetes?

### The Challenge: Initial Data Seeding

When you spin up a mirrored cluster with the IKO, you get a clean slate. But most real-world scenarios require moving existing data into this new environment. Traditionally, this involved complex manual steps—moving `.dat` files, mounting PVCs, and configuring mirroring.

### Step 1: Storage Configuration

We start by ensuring our storage classes are correctly defined to support the performance and persistence requirements of our database.

![Storage Class Definition](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12095).png)

Once defined, we can verify that our Persistent Volume Claims (PVCs) are correctly provisioned and ready for data.

![PVC Status Check](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12096).png)

### Step 2: Seeding the Databases

The IKO simplifies the process of creating and mirroring databases. We define our databases in the `IrisCluster` manifest, and the operator ensures they are created and added to the mirror set across all members.

![Mirror Databases View](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12098).png)

Are they mirrored? 🎉 They ARE!

![Mirror Check Success](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12100).png)

### Step 3: Verifying the Topology

We can also check if our ECP (Enterprise Cache Protocol) clients are correctly connected to our new mirrored data members.

![ECP Client Connection](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12102).png)

### Attestation: The Force in Action

Let's prove the data is flowing correctly across the entire cluster. We'll set a global on the Primary member:

![Setting a Global](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12101).png)

```cls
Set ^MayTheForce="Be With You"
```

Now, we use a simple script to loop through all pods in the namespace and verify the global exists on every member—primary, backup, async DR, and even accessible via the compute nodes.

```bash
# seedmirrorcheck.sh snippet
for pod in $(kubectl get pods -o jsonpath='{.items[*].metadata.name}'); do
  kubectl exec "$pod" -- iris session IRIS -U ENSDEMO "ZWRITE ^MayTheForce"
done
```

The results are solid:

![Mirror Seeding Verification Results](https://community.intersystems.com/sites/default/files/inline/images/images/image(12103).png)

- ✅ **Primary**: Persisted
- ✅ **Backup**: Persisted
- ✅ **Async DR**: Persisted
- ✅ **Compute**: Accessible

🎉 The force is definitely with us.

### Summary

Seeding data into a mirrored IRIS cluster on Kubernetes doesn't have to be a "square dance" of manual steps. By leveraging the IKO's declarative management, you can ensure your databases are correctly provisioned, mirrored, and synchronized across your entire global topology from day one.
